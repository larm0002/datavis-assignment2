Meteorite Impacts on Earth

Most meteorites are bits of debris broken off from comets, asteroids, and planets. They are called meteoroids whilst still in space, meteors once they’ve entered Earth’s atmosphere, and meteorites if they make it to the surface. Travelling through Earth’s atmosphere, meteors burn up and disintegrate, usually only retaining 5% or less of their original mass. They range from rock to metallic materials, including combinations of the two, and it’s estimated that around 44,000 kg of meteoritic material reach Earth’s surface each day, but these meteorites are rarely large enough to cause any harm. Meteorites can cause significant damage though, as seen by the impact that left behind a 300-km-wide crater and caused the dinosaur extinction event.
